Welcome To Dexcom

For 10 weeks, I worked alongside peers at The Design Lab at UCSD as part of the Diabetes Design Initiative. My team and I worked with Dexcom to figure out the best way to deliver a welcome series of content for their products and how to deliver it.

UX Designer, UX Researcher

User Research, Journey Mapping, Prototyping, Testing, Wireframing, Figma, Miro

Apr - Jun 2020 (10 weeks)

Background —

Dexcom is a company that produces continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems to help those with diabetes track and manage their glucose levels.

The CGM is a device that attaches to a patient’s body and continuously reads glucose levels every 5 minutes and transmits the information in real time to a receiver or smart device.

Diabetes patients need to be able to have access to their readings to understand their glucose levels and administer insulin as necessary and maintain their health. Improper management of glucose levels can lead to dangerous health complications.

Problem —

How can we show that Dexcom cares for users at all points in the troubleshooting journey?

Since Dexcom diabetes patients rely heavily on their CGM to maintain their health, it can be very stressful and anxiety inducing for them when they are having issues with their CGM, preventing them from accessing their readings.

“ Because of the way the pump is keeping me alive, I like calling when something goes wrong. Need it to be fixed right away, need to be able to trust the fix is working ”



We wanted to provide self support resources to users when they run into issues at any point in their journey.

What I Did —

  • Continuously researched to understand user pain points and compile a user journey map.
  • Conducted interviews and tests with Dexcom CGM users.
  • Planned and ran weekly mentor meetings, consisting of workshops and critique sessions, with Dexcom and DDI mentors to ensure clear understanding of company needs and expectations.
  • Constructed final deliverable presentations to Dexcom representatives to receive design critiques and update the company on the next steps of the design process.
  • Continuously ideated, prototyped, and tested multiple design ideas to generate a solution that would meet all needs of the stakeholders.
