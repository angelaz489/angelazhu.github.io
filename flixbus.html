Flixbus Redesign

This project was started in a design class at UCSD within a group of 4 people. The goal was to analyze a major travel site, access the top problems of the site, and choose a component to redesign in order to solve the chosen problems.

UX Designer, UX Researcher

User Research, Information Architecture, Prototyping, Testing, Wireframing, Figma

Nov 2019 (2 weeks)

Challenge

Redesign to solve an issue with the lack of help and documentation of the website.

Deliverables

Mockups exibiting redesign and new interactions.

Background —

FlixBus is a bus service that provides intercity transportation to over 100 million people with over 2000 destinations across 30 countries.

FlixBus Mission: “Green and Smart Mobility for Everyone to Experience the World”.

With a focus on the digitalization of a traditional bus industry, Flixbus provides solutions such as a mobile FlixBus application, e-ticketing, and “Where’s My Bus” GPS live tracking.

Research —

After reviewing the FlixBus website, my team and I came up with a couple tasks to have users complete during our usability tests that allowed the users to be able to experience the overall site. The tasks were created to see how users would interact with the features on the website, to see user’s thought process while navigating the site, and to see what aspects of the site give users trouble.‍



We interviewed and conducted usability tests on 4 people who all happened to not have had any previous experience with FlixBus. The test consisted of tasks such as:

  1. Booking rides based on different locations and situations
  2. Managing bookings
  3. Finding information to answer frequently asked questions

Findings —

All four participants faced issues with choosing pick up and drop off locations of the bus because of the lack of Help and Documentation.

The users were able to easily enter and select a city. However, in many cases each city contains multiple bus stops. The website does not provide a clear understanding for the users of where each bus stop is. As shown from the tasks, my group understood that people who were booking buses to places they were not familiar with did not understand where some bus stations were and whether or not they were close to their destination.

Goals —

Provide users with more information on bus station locations.
