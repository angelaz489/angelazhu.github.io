University of CGM

For 10 weeks, I worked alongside peers at The Design Lab at UCSD as part of the Diabetes Design Initiative. My team and I worked with Dexcom to empower the transition towards adulthood for people with diabetes while simultaneously strengthening the relationship between Dexcom and the College Diabetes Network (CDN).

UX Designer, UX Researcher

User Research, Journey Mapping, Prototyping, Testing, Wireframing, Figma

Jan - Mar 2020 (10 weeks)

Background —

Dexcom is a company that produces continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems to help those with diabetes track and manage their glucose levels.

The CGM is a device that attaches to a patient’s body and continuously reads glucose levels every 5 minutes and transmits the information in real time to a receiver or smart device.

Diabetes patients need to be able to have access to their readings to understand their glucose levels and administer insulin as necessary and maintain their health.

Problem —

How might we empower students with T1D in the classroom while strengthening the relationship between Dexcom and CDN?

Young adults with Type 1 diabetes, transitioning into adulthood, need help with advocating for themselves in various social situations.

Specifically, students are finding it difficult to take care of their health while going through their rigorous and hectic school schedules.



“ This was the first time I was really all on my own. ”



We aimed to empower students with Type 1 diabetes by finding a solution that would allow them to confidently advocate for themselves while maintaining control of their health.

What I Did —

  • Conducted interviews with Dexcom CGM users and continuously did second hand research to understand user pain points and compile a user journey map.
  • Regularly reached out to Dexcom representatives to ensure clear understanding of company needs and expectations.
  • Maintained communication with College Diabetes Network representatives to be conscious of their limitations and expectations.
  • Constructed work-in-progress and final deliverable presentations to Dexcom design team to receive design critiques and update the company on the next steps of the design process.
  • Continuously ideated, prototyped, and tested multiple design ideas to generate a solution that would meet all needs of the stakeholders.
